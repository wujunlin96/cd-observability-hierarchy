# External AI review triage v132

Date: 2026-07-01

Purpose: record the second external-AI review and separate immediate manuscript repairs from mandatory later calculations. The review is useful but partly overstates what the present paper claims; the repairs below keep the manuscript clean while preserving the issues for the journal path.

## Addressed in the manuscript now

1. Abstract directness. The abstract now opens with the core question: whether a hidden sector with speed scale \(D\) leaves an invariant imprint on a visible sector with speed scale \(C\).
2. Symbol clarity. The introduction now says \(C\) and \(D\) are speed scales, not coordinates or manifold labels.
3. Nonlinear point maps. Theorem 2 now explicitly says it is not a theorem about DSR or relative locality; it only covers ordinary spacetime point maps \(F:V\to V\).
4. Causality. Section 6 now states that negative \(\Delta t\) is only a kinematic diagnostic and that any two-interface model needs a preferred wall foliation, global time function, or equivalent restriction to avoid controllable closed causal curves.
5. Wall thickness. Section 6.1 now notes that \(L_w\) must come from wall-field mass, defect tension, plasma/material response, or another dynamical scale in a microscopic model.
6. High-energy photon constraints. Section 6.2 now warns that photon-like implementations need a time-of-flight and spectral recast; the toy model is not a claim of an unsuppressed cosmological photon speed shift.
7. SME matching. Sections 4 and 7 now clarify that the paper gives a matching requirement, not a completed SME coefficient dictionary.
8. Radiative stability. Section 8 now identifies radiative feed-through of visible Lorentz violation as the most serious missing dynamical issue.

## Not solved yet

1. No explicit SME coefficient map has been computed.
2. No one-loop radiative correction or technical-naturalness calculation has been performed.
3. No four-dimensional photon, fermion, or gravitational interface scattering calculation has been performed.
4. No gamma-ray burst, Fermi-LAT-like, or other high-energy time-of-flight recast has been performed.
5. No microscopic wall model fixes \(L_w\) from a field mass, tension, plasma scale, or cosmological defect network.
6. No full chronology-protection theorem exists for multi-interface configurations with \(D>C\).

## Decision

The first arXiv manuscript can remain a no-go/classification paper if these stronger claims are not made. A journal version should choose one route, preferably interface/holonomy or low-rank SME matching, and close the relevant items above with actual calculations.
