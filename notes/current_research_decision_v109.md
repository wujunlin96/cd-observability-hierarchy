# Current research decision v109

Date: 2026-06-30

## Decision

Continue the project, but with a changed claim.

Do not claim:

> We discovered or predicted a directly visible second light speed.

Claim instead:

> A hidden C-D speed ratio is locally unobservable under exact relativity, smooth sector maps, and flat relative calibration. Observable C-D physics requires a controlled failure mode. The paper classifies those failure modes and works out one interface example.

## Relativity-principle answer

Relativity-principle breaking is possible, but the acceptable C-D version is narrow:

\[
\text{visible local Lorentz relativity protected}
\quad+\quad
\text{hidden or relative calibration allowed to fail}
\quad+\quad
\text{observability portal-suppressed or defect/interface-limited}.
\]

Large visible-sector breaking is not a good route. It immediately enters SME, clock, photon, neutrino, and gravitational-wave constraints.

## Current strongest result

The strongest result is the no-go hierarchy:

1. constant Lorentz-compatible local maps are only scale maps;
2. smooth Lorentz-equivariant point maps preserve null rays;
3. flat relative calibration connections are pure gauge;
4. therefore physical C-D observability begins at preferred fields, holonomy/curvature, defects/interfaces, or portal-projected EFT coefficients.

## Current weakness
