Date: 2026-06-30

Purpose:

## Executive verdict

The original "two different light speeds" intuition is not publishable as a new physical effect by itself. Under exact local relativity and smooth sector calibration it is a convention/scale-map statement.

The remaining publishable core is narrower:

> A theorem-driven observability hierarchy for multi-sector speed constants: exact relativity removes local C-D speed observability; nonlinear point maps do not help; flat relative calibration connections are pure gauge; observable C-D physics first appears only through preferred fields, relative holonomy/curvature, defects/interfaces, or portal-projected EFT coefficients.

This is not a discovery paper. It is a short theoretical classification/no-go paper with one worked escape example.

## External gates that must appear in any paper

### Gate 1: SME gate

The SME already tabulates matter, photon, neutrino, and gravity-sector Lorentz/CPT-violation coefficients. The 2026 update of the Data Tables is the current constraint index. Any local visible-sector C-D breaking must either map into SME coefficients or explicitly explain why it does not.

Consequence:

> Never write "visible C and D light cones differ macroscopically" without an SME mapping.

### Gate 2: Disformal/aether gate

Bekenstein already established the broad disformal relation between physical and gravitational geometries. Einstein-aether theory already studies a dynamical unit timelike vector field and its observational constraints.

Consequence:

> A hidden disformal metric is an allowed modeling move, not a new invention.

### Gate 3: Photon velocity-oscillation gate

Glashow's photon/paraphoton velocity-mixing model already constrains a second massless photon with Lorentz-noninvariant velocity mixing from CMB distortion at roughly \(10^{-32}c\). De Angelis and Pain strengthen this using distant sources.

Consequence:

> Photon portal is a constraint appendix, not a discovery route.

### Gate 4: Neutrino altered-dispersion gate

The SME neutrino formalism already includes unconventional energy dependences in oscillation lengths and mixings. Shortcut/ADR sterile-neutrino models already produce active-sterile resonance structures from geometric or dispersion differences.

Consequence:

> A C-D neutrino phase is not new unless we prove a restrictive low-rank texture and perform a real data recast.

### Gate 5: Weyl/nonmetricity/clock gate

Weyl second-clock effects and their experimental status are already debated. Atomic-clock networks and GPS have already been used to search for topological defects/domain walls.

Consequence:

> A C-D holonomy route survives only as a relative hidden-sector calibration observable with suppressed portal and a morphology not identical to generic clock drift.

### Gate 6: Gravity/bimetric gate

Generic bimetric dynamics have ghost and consistency problems unless built carefully, e.g. Hassan--Rosen-type ghost-free structures. GW170817/GRB170817A constrains visible gravity-light speed differences at about \(10^{-15}\).

Consequence:

> Do not enter full multimetric gravity in the current paper except as a limitation.

## Publishable minimal paper claim

The clean claim should be:

> We formulate a C-D multi-sector speed hypothesis as an observability problem. Under exact Lorentz relativity, smooth local sector maps, and flat relative calibration, a hidden speed ratio \(D/C\) has no local invariant content as a visible second cone. Observable C-D physics requires failure of at least one assumption, and the allowed failures are sharply constrained by existing SME, disformal/aether, Weyl/nonmetricity, defect, and bimetric literature. A finite interface model illustrates one controlled escape, with conversion set by the wall Fourier profile at \(k_C-k_D\).

This is modest but real.

## What not to claim

Do not claim:

1. discovery of a second speed;
2. a new kind of Lorentz violation in photons;
3. a new active-sterile neutrino oscillation phase;
4. a new Weyl-like second clock effect;
5. a complete bimetric/gravity theory;
6. experimental evidence.
